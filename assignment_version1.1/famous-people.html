Tim Berners Lee



Tim Berners Lee was a talented computer scientist who contributed a lot to the field of computer science and invented the World Wide Web, which is known to everyone. He will be introduced below in terms of both his biography and his contributions to the field of computer science.

Biography

Tim Berners was born in 1955 in London, England, and entered Oxford University in 1976 to study physics. In 1989, while working at the European Center for Nuclear Research (CERN), he came up with the idea of the World Wide Web, and so he wrote the first World Wide Web server and client. Between 1991 and 1993, he continued to test and modify the design of the World Wide Web, and developed the standards of the HyperText Markup Language (HTML), Uniform Resource Identifier (URI), and Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) (Tim Berners-Lee. 2023). These standards are still widely used today.In 1994, Tim founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), an organization formed to standardize the protocols and standards of the World Wide Web (Berners-Lee et al. 1994). In 2016, Tim received the Turing Award.

Evaluation

Overall, the World Wide Web is a very important part of computer science, it is a brand new way of communicating and sharing information, people can upload and share their own knowledge through the World Wide Web, and they can also access other people's knowledge, which is obviously much more efficient than obtaining information from traditional libraries. Berners-Lee undoubtedly contributed to the popularization of the Internet and profoundly influenced the way people acquired knowledge. At the same time, Berners-Lee also developed standards for HTTP, URI, and Html, making the World Wide Web more easily to develop and maintain.

Contributions to computer



1. World Wide Web

In 1990, Tim invented the World Wide Web (Berners-Lee 1999) , before the World Wide Web, the way people accessed knowledge was single and inefficient, for example, to read a book, you had to read the whole book to know the whole content, but the World Wide Web introduced hypertext links to connect all the knowledge together by clicking on the links to access the web pages. In this way, knowledge distribution and access is totally changed, and people can quickly find effective content and ignore unnecessary content.

2. HTTP

Tim et al. also designed Internet standard tracking protocol(HTTP) for the Internet community(Nielsen etal., 1999), which defines the rules for transferring information between a client and a server.

3. URI

Tim et al. have developed a standard for Uniform Resource Identifier (URI) (Berners-Lee et al. 2005) that can help people locate resources in the Internet.

4. HTML

Tim was one of the creator of HTML, which is used to define the structure and content of web pages.
